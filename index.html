The Problem

You're at Stanford. You Feel Like You're Falling Behind.

Here's what every freshman is secretly thinking — and none of it is true.

😰

"Everyone else has their major figured out."

They don't. 70% of Stanford students change their major at least once in the first two years.

📅

"I need 3 internships, research, and a startup."

Spreading thin leads to burnout. Depth in one area beats shallow breadth every time on a résumé.

🔕

"I don't know what I'm actually good at."

That's normal — and fixable. Self-knowledge is a skill, and CoreQuest is designed to build it fast.

📱

"My LinkedIn feed makes me feel inadequate."

Social media shows highlight reels. The antidote is knowing your own reel — not envying someone else's.

How It Works

Four Steps to Your Core

From self-reflection to real-world clarity in under two hours.

1

Take the Aptitude Quiz 🧠

A 8-question personality + aptitude test maps your cognitive style, values, and natural strengths to academic and career clusters. Takes 4 minutes.

2

Choose Your Escape Room 🗝️

Pick from six themed rooms — each one mirrors a real academic discipline. The puzzles are designed by faculty and career coaches at Stanford.

3

Play & Discover 🔍

Spend 60–75 min solving puzzles with 2–5 teammates. Our facilitators observe how you think, lead, and collaborate under pressure.

4

Get Your Core Profile 📊

Receive a personalized report: your archetype, top major paths, matching career roles, and a "one thing to focus on this quarter" action card.
